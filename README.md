Prompting Techniques and Examples
1. Comparative Analysis Prompt
This technique asks the AI to compare two or more subjects based on specific criteria. It's great for competitive analysis and benchmarking.
•	Example Prompt: "Act as a market research analyst. Compare and contrast InnovateTech's new loyalty program with 'Apex Retail's' established rewards system. Your analysis should cover the following points: enrollment process, reward structure, customer engagement metrics (hypothetical), and overall value proposition for the customer. Present the findings in a side-by-side table."


3. Experiential Perspective Prompt
This technique instructs the AI to adopt a specific persona to generate a response from a particular point of view. It's useful for gaining insights into a user's journey or a stakeholder's mindset.
•	Example Prompt: "Act as a customer who has just enrolled in the InnovateTech loyalty program. Write a short journal entry detailing your experience. What were your first impressions? Was the sign-up process easy? How do you feel about the rewards? What would you change to make the experience better?"


4. Everyday Functioning Prompts
This technique focuses on generating practical, actionable content for a specific daily or operational task. It's ideal for creating functional documentation, step-by-step guides, or internal memos.
•	Example Prompt: "Write a concise, internal memo for the marketing team at InnovateTech. The memo should summarize the initial performance data of the loyalty program and list three actionable, data-driven recommendations to improve customer retention in the next quarter."


5. Universal Prompt Structures
This is a comprehensive, structured approach that combines various elements to create a highly specific and effective prompt. It typically includes: Role, Task, Context, Format, and Constraints.
•	Example Prompt: "### Role: You are the head of the data analysis department at InnovateTech. ### Task: Develop a detailed report on the initial performance of the new customer loyalty program. ### Context: The report is for the company's executive board. It should highlight key performance indicators (KPIs) such as customer enrollment rates, reward redemption frequency, and retention rate over the first 30 days. ### Format: The report must be a professional document with an executive summary, a data analysis section using bullet points, and a concluding section with clear recommendations. ### Constraints: The entire report must not exceed 600 words and must maintain a formal, objective tone."


6. Prompt Refinements and Size Limitations
This involves the iterative process of fine-tuning a prompt and understanding the constraints of the AI's context window. Prompt refinement is about starting with a broad idea and adding specificity to get a better output. Prompt size limitations refer to the maximum number of tokens (words, punctuation, etc.) an AI model can process in a single prompt. If a prompt is too long, the AI may cut off the input or generate a less coherent response.
•	Refinement Process:
o	Initial Prompt: "Write a report on our new loyalty program."
o	Refined Prompt: "Write a report on our new loyalty program, focusing on its first-month performance. Include enrollment numbers and customer feedback."
o	Final Prompt (incorporating all techniques): "Act as a data analyst. Analyze the first 30 days of the InnovateTech loyalty program. The report should include an executive summary, a bulleted list of three key performance metrics (enrollment, redemption, retention), and a section with three actionable recommendations. The tone should be formal and professional. The target audience is the executive leadership team. The report must be under 500 words."

Key Takeaways
•	Prompting matters: The quality of the output is directly proportional to the quality and specificity of the prompt.
•	AI tools vary: Different AI models have varying capabilities. More advanced models are better at understanding and adhering to complex instructions, especially those involving multiple constraints and a specific persona.
•	Iterate and refine: Prompt engineering is an iterative process. By refining your prompts, you can guide the AI to generate more accurate, relevant, and well-structured outputs.
•	Mind the size: While a well-structured, detailed prompt is key, it's crucial to stay within the model's token limit to ensure it processes the entire request. Too much information can lead to confusion or truncation.
